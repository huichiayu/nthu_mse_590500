The gas in a modern day gasoline enegine follow the process of Carnot cycle (1824 by Carnot, Nicolas Lé onard Sadi Carnot, 1796– 1832). In the figure below, the cyclic process includes

  1. isothermal expansion from A to B.

  2. adiabatic expansion form B to C.

  3. isothermal compression from C to D. (negative)

  4. adiabatic compression from D to A. (negiative)

As such, the area enclosed by the loop is the work done by the cyclic process.

\[W_{Carnot} = W_{AB} + W_{BC} + W_{CD} + W_{DA}\]
